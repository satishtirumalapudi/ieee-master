Who Are We ?

IEEE-CBIT is a cutting edge platform to enhance and develop eminent skill sets on the upcoming technologies of the rising technical community. IEEE CBIT consists of two societies namely Power and Energy Society (PES) and Computer Society (CS). We also have an affinity group, Women In Engineering (WIE). IEEE-CBIT won the prestigious ‘Upcoming Student Branch’ award in Hyderabad Section Student Congress held in December 2017. Student branch deals with the internal logistics and operations under supervision of an executive body formed every year by the students in the college. Each and every decision made by the executive body stays transparent without any veto power incongruous to the practices of anarchy. Entire student body is segregated into various line of works which are to be embarked on the interest basis of the members. Our student branch is one of the most active branches in hyderabad section. IEEE-CBIT student branch strives every year in fulfilling the IEEE global community’s vision and maintain healthy relations with the student network team for a better and productive future. Established in 1993, our student branch provided innumerable opportunities to the members and fellow students on their journey to excellence. Till date, IEEE CBIT conducted ample of events, workshops and prolific activities. Many students shaped up their career by doing research and publishing papers on divergent fields via IEEE-CBIT. Vision, mission and exhibition are three pillars of IEEE-CBIT firmly embedded on our very foundation.

Visit To Srisailam 

             	    	
Event title:Visit to Srisailam.
Event category:IEEE PES 
Date:IEEE PES .
Objective:The purpose of this Industrial visit was to provide an excellent opportunity to all the students & faculties, to interact and know more about industrial environment. The intention was to help the students to create a practical perspective on the World of Engineering.
Event Description:The Srisailam Dam is a dam constructed across the Krishna River at Srisailam in the Kurnool district in the state of Andhra Pradesh in India and is the 3rd largest capacity hydroelectric project in the country.
The dam was constructed in a deep gorge in the Nallamala Hills in Kurnool district, 300 m (980 ft) above sea level. It is 512 m (1,680 ft) long, 269.748 m (885.00 ft) high and has 12 radial crest gates. It has a reservoir of 800 km2 (310 sq mi). The left bank power station houses 6 × 150 MW   reversible Francis-pump turbines (for pumped-storage) and the right bank contains 7 × 110 MW Francis-turbine generators.
The power generated is around 150MW these generators are connected in parallel. These individual generators are connected to transformers of rating 190MVA. These transformers step up the voltage from 13.8kv to 400kv. These secondary windings of transformer which are at 400kv are connected to a switch yard with the help of underground XLPE cables. 
In switch yard CTs and PTs are connected to monitor the currents, voltages, power factors and load
